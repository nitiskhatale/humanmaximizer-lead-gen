Why LangGraph — The State Machine
1:10 – 1:45
🖥️
Open backend/agents/graph.py — highlight the build_graph() function and _route_after_qualification()
"I used LangGraph to wire the three agents together. Let me show you why this matters.

Look at this build_graph function. Three nodes, three edges. The critical line is this conditional edge after Agent 2.

If the lead is qualified, it goes to the Sales Agent. If it is not qualified, it goes straight to END — no outreach is generated, no LLM tokens are wasted.

That routing decision is a Python if-statement — not an LLM call. That is the whole point of LangGraph. Your pipeline logic is code, not prompts. Deterministic, fast, auditable."

Agent 1 — Research Agent
1:45 – 2:30
🖥️
Open backend/agents/research_agent.py — scroll to _run_live() and _DIRECTORY_URL_KEYWORDS
"Agent 1 is the Research Agent. Here is what it does step by step.

First, it calls SerpAPI with your keyword and location — gives us Google search results programmatically.

Second, it filters out directory sites. You can see this list — Glassdoor, Naukri, IndiaMart, Crunchbase. We skip these. We want the company's actual website, not an aggregator.

Third, it scrapes the company's website using BeautifulSoup and extracts: employee count using regex, HQ location, industry, and HR decision-maker contacts — names, titles, emails, LinkedIn URLs.

Finally, it calls Mistral-7B to generate a three-paragraph company intelligence report. Company overview, HR pain points, and HRMS fit signal."

Agent 2 — Qualification Agent
2:30 – 3:15
🖥️
Open backend/agents/qualification_agent.py — show the 5 scoring functions: score_company_size, score_industry, score_tech_stack, score_dm_reachability, score_growth_signal
"Agent 2 is the Qualification Agent. Here I made a key design decision — I score leads with deterministic Python functions, not an LLM.

Five dimensions, each worth 0 to 20 points.

Company size — under 50 employees scores 2, over 2000 scores 20. That is the enterprise buyer profile.

Industry — manufacturing, healthcare, BFSI are primary targets, 20 points. Unknown industry gets 6.

Tech stack gap — this is the most important one. On Excel or spreadsheets? 18 points — strongest buying signal. Already on Darwinbox or Keka? Only 4 points — hard to displace an incumbent.

Decision maker reachability — email plus LinkedIn is 20. No contact found is 0.

Growth signal — hiring surge or recent funding is 20. Contracting is 2.

Total out of 100. If the score is below 35, or if less than 40% of the data fields have real values, the lead is disqualified and the Sales Agent never runs. This saves LLM tokens on bad leads.

After scoring, Mistral-7B writes exactly two sentences: the best positive signal and the biggest risk."
6
Agent 3 — Sales Agent & RAG
3:15 – 4:00
🖥️
Go back to docs/architecture.md — scroll to the RAG Pipeline section of the diagram
"Agent 3 is the Sales Agent. It only runs for qualified leads.

It uses RAG — Retrieval Augmented Generation. Here is the idea: if you ask Mistral-7B to write a cold email about HumanMaximizer's features, it will make things up. It does not know what HumanMaximizer actually sells.

So I pre-crawled humanmaximizer.com, split all the product content into small chunks, embedded each chunk into a vector using nomic-embed-text, and stored them in ChromaDB — a local vector database.

When the Sales Agent writes an email for a hospital chain, it queries ChromaDB for the most relevant product knowledge — attendance, compliance, payroll features — retrieves the top 5 matching chunks, and tells Mistral-7B: write the email using only these facts.

Every claim in the email is grounded in real product content. No hallucination. And the retrieved chunks are saved in the lead record so you can verify every sentence."
