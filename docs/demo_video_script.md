## Script

### [0:00 – 0:45] Introduction

> "Hi, I'm [name]. This is my submission for the Razor Infotech AI Architect assignment — an autonomous AI Lead Generation platform built for HumanMaximizer HRMS.

> The platform takes a simple business keyword and location, and runs a fully autonomous 3-agent pipeline that discovers companies, qualifies them as HRMS prospects, and writes personalised outreach — entirely without manual input.

> The stack is: FastAPI, LangGraph for the agent orchestration, Mistral-7B running locally via Ollama, ChromaDB for RAG-grounded outreach, and SQLite with Alembic migrations. Let me show you how it works."

**[Screen: Streamlit Home page]**

---

### [0:45 – 1:30] Architecture Overview

> "On the Home page of the Streamlit dashboard, you can see the overall system.

> There are 3 agents in a LangGraph pipeline:
> - The **Research Agent** searches Google via SerpAPI, scrapes the company website, extracts decision makers using regex pattern matching, and calls Mistral-7B to generate a 3-paragraph company intelligence report.
> - The **Qualification Agent** runs 5 deterministic scoring functions — no LLM involvement in the scoring itself, so results are reproducible. It then generates a 2-sentence qualification reasoning with Mistral-7B.
> - The **Sales Agent** only runs for qualified leads. It retrieves the top 5 relevant product knowledge chunks from ChromaDB, then generates a RAG-grounded cold email and a LinkedIn message.

> The scoring threshold is 35 out of 100. Anything below that is automatically disqualified. The conditional edge in LangGraph handles this routing."

**[Screen: Show the pipeline steps section on Home page]**

---

### [1:30 – 3:30] Live Lead Search

> "Let me run the pipeline live. I'll go to the Find Leads page."

**[Navigate to Find Leads in Streamlit]**

> "I'll use one of the preset examples — Manufacturing companies in Pune, India — and request 3 leads."

**[Click 'Manufacturing · Pune' preset, set max_leads to 3, click Launch AI Agent Pipeline]**

> "You can see the chain-of-thought trace as the agents execute in real time. The Research Agent is running first — initialising the SerpAPI search, scraping company websites, running ContactFinder to identify HR decision makers, then calling Mistral-7B for the research summary.

> Next the Qualification Agent scores each company across 5 dimensions — company size fit, industry relevance, tech stack gap, decision maker reachability, and growth signal.

> Then the Sales Agent retrieves product knowledge from ChromaDB and generates the outreach.

> This is live Mistral-7B inference — no hardcoded responses, everything generated at runtime."

**[Wait for results to appear]**

> "The results come back ranked by qualification score. Let me click into the top lead to see the full details."

**[Click the expander for the top result]**

### [3:30 – 5:00] Deep Dive: Score Breakdown and Outreach

**[Show Score Breakdown tab]**

> "Here's the 5-dimensional score breakdown. You can see the radar chart visualising the lead's profile — high industry relevance, strong growth signal, and a tech stack gap because they're on Excel. The qualification confidence shows how complete the data is.

> The qualification reasoning on the left is generated by Mistral-7B — two sentences explaining the strongest signal and the biggest risk."

**[Click Outreach Drafts tab]**

> "This is the RAG-grounded cold email. Every claim in this email is traceable to the HumanMaximizer knowledge base — you can see the RAG chunks that were retrieved and injected into the prompt. The hallucination prevention is 3-layered: the prompt explicitly forbids inventing facts, the chunks are stored as an audit trail on the lead, and there's an automated self-critique tool that runs a second LLM pass to verify claims.

> Below the email is the LinkedIn message — under 300 characters, no emojis, personalised to the decision maker found in the research phase."

**[Click Company Intelligence tab]**

> "The Company Intelligence tab shows the full 3-paragraph Mistral-7B research summary — Company Overview, HR Pain Points, and HRMS Fit Signal."

### [6:00 – 7:00] RAG Knowledge Base

**[Switch to Streamlit, navigate to Knowledge Base page]**

> "The RAG knowledge base is built from humanmaximizer.com. The WebIngestor crawls the site to depth 3, chunks the content into 512-token segments with 64-token overlap, embeds them with nomic-embed-text through Ollama, and stores them in ChromaDB.

> Let me test the retrieval directly."

**[Type: "payroll compliance multi-state manufacturing" in the query box, click Search ChromaDB]**

> "These are the exact chunks that would be injected into the cold email prompt for a manufacturing company. Every email is grounded in these actual product facts — the RAG pipeline ensures outreach is specific and credible, not generic."

---

### [7:00 – 7:45] Observability

**[Open new tab, navigate to localhost:8000/metrics]**

> "The Prometheus metrics endpoint is live. You can see `leads_processed_total` segmented by qualified, disqualified, and error status. `lead_qualification_score` tracks the distribution of scores across all leads. `rag_chunks_retrieved` monitors ChromaDB health. And `hallucination_checks_total` tracks the self-critique results — the ratio of grounded to ungrounded outreach.

> When `LANGCHAIN_API_KEY` is configured, every LangGraph node is traced in LangSmith — per-agent token usage, latency, and full prompt visibility for debugging."

### [8:30 – 9:00] Closing

> - A 3-agent LangGraph pipeline with deterministic qualification scoring and RAG-grounded outreach generation

> - 5 Jinja2 prompt templates with StrictUndefined mode and explicit anti-hallucination instructions
